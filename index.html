What's a Digital Footprint?

According to the Webster Dictionary a digital footprint is "the information about a particular person that exists on the Internet as a result of their online activity."

There are two main classifications of a digital footprint:

  • Passive: Where data is collected without the owner knowing.
    This has started many debates about online privacy among the user.

  • Active: Where personal data is released intentionally by a user for the purpose of sharing information about oneself by means of websites or social media.

The data collected from the use wheather passively or actively is stored in large databases. Many times your data is sold to other companies that use that data to market items for you to buy.

There is an upside for companies to gather this data. On many websites it is used to filter content that you would find uninteresting, boring and recommend content that you would enjoy.
